## One-paragraph truth

v0.3 grows the atlas as a **workbench**: it adds reusable, importable formal
capital, not new survey-row coverage. The survey's 44 rows are the seed, not the
yardstick — the value this cycle is what a downstream Lean user can now `import`
and build on. New in 0.3: a native No Free Lunch module (BY-021 optimization +
BY-020 supervised) carrying five proofs — including an adaptive no-revisit NFL
and a loss-vector-level homogeneity `iff` — importable via
`AISafetyAtlas.Learning`; the vendored classical Gibbard–Satterthwaite proof
built at the atlas 4.31 toolchain and importable via `AISafetyAtlas.SocialChoice`
(`LAND-GS-002`); and the first **possibility**-lane entry, the DeepMind
doubly-efficient debate, reproduced (`LAND-DEBATE-001`, upstream v4.8) alongside
the SSBD PAC No-Free-Lunch lower bound (`LAND-NFL-001`, Isabelle). For the record
on the *survey* axis: headline `EXACT`/`EQUIVALENT` coverage is unchanged at
**7 of 44** and no AI-safety bridge graduated (both NFL rows remain
`ai_bridge_status: HUMAN_REVIEW`) — but that axis does not gate the importable
capital above. The NFL artifacts are `NEW_PROOF`/`RELATED`: they reproduce the
papers' deterministic finite cases exactly and do not claim EXACT/EQUIVALENT
fidelity to the full (stochastic/non-uniform-prior) papers.

## Critical non-claims

- **NEW_PROOF ≠ new headline coverage.** The NFL artifacts are `RELATED` to the
  full Wolpert / Wolpert–Macready papers; they reproduce the deterministic
  finite-case claims exactly but do not cover stochastic learners/algorithms,
  label noise, non-uniform target priors, or time-varying objectives.
- **The homogeneity `iff` is a loss-*vector*-level characterization.**
  `homogeneous_iff_learner_indep` states homogeneity ⟺ learner-independence of
  *every functional of the OTS loss vector*. Necessity from the weaker *scalar*
  total-loss distribution alone is **not** claimed and may fail for `|Sᶜ| > 1`.
- **The `iff` is a `NEW_PROOF` (folklore-tight), not novel mathematics.** Wolpert
  never stated it as a biconditional; it is the loss-axis analog of the
  Schumacher–Vose–Whitley / Igel–Toussaint closed-under-permutation
  characterization (prior axis), which remains an optional reproduction target
  (CT-10).
- **No AI-safety bridge graduated.** Both NFL rows stay `HUMAN_REVIEW`; no
  bridge theorem consumes the NFL results yet. v0.3 adds mathematical capital,
  not a new safety claim.
- **Debate and SSBD are landscape reproductions**, not in-atlas survey-row
  headline coverage. Debate has no in-tree import surface yet (Path B deferred
  until a downstream consumer needs it).
